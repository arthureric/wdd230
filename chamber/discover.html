Shoshone, Idaho

  • Founded in 1882
  • Population 1,502
  • Elevation 3,963 feet
  • Four major highways converge in Shoshone

Shoshone is a small farming community located in central Idaho. Shoshone is the country seat of Lincoln county.

Shoshone was origanl built as a railway stop. It was the only location in souther Idaho you get on an amtrak train.

With the decrease in nation rail travel the local economies growth has shifted more towards agriculture.

The Sawtooth market has served the local communitiy for decades.

Shoshone boast a local hardware store as well as several antique shops.

Once a center for volcanic activity the local area covered with ancient lava tubes. The Shoshone ice caves and the Mammoth cave to name a few.

Family owned farms dot the landscape and produce a wide variety of crops.

Some nearby attractions are black magic canyon and the Craters of the Moon National Monument.

Cattle and sheep producion are woven in the life here. Several large scale dairy operations call Lincoln county home.
